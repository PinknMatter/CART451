Week 3

CrowdSourcing

"The Sheep Market" by Aaron Koblin and Lauren McCarthy's "Social Turkers" project both exemplify the principles of crowdsourcing discussed by Jeff Howe and Lauren Lee Mccarthy. Koblin's project, centered around crowdsourced art (specifically, drawings of sheep), mirrors Howe's notion of distributing traditionally individual tasks to a diverse crowd for collective creativity. Similarly, "Social Turkers" explores the diversity of human interactions in a digital context, showcasing the collaborative potential of technology-mediated social tasks. These projects underscore the power of crowdsourcing to engage individuals from various backgrounds in creative and collaborative endeavors, while also challenging conventional boundaries between work, art, and social interaction in the digital age.
