Week 3

CrowdSourcing

"The Sheep Market" by Aaron Koblin and Lauren McCarthy's "Social Turkers" project both exemplify the principles of crowdsourcing discussed by Jeff Howe and Lauren Lee Mccarthy. Koblin's project, centered around crowdsourced art (specifically, drawings of sheep), mirrors Howe's notion of distributing traditionally individual tasks to a diverse crowd for collective creativity. Similarly, "Social Turkers" explores the diversity of human interactions in a digital context, showcasing the collaborative potential of technology-mediated social tasks. These projects underscore the power of crowdsourcing to engage individuals from various backgrounds in creative and collaborative endeavors, while also challenging conventional boundaries between work, art, and social interaction in the digital age.

Week 6

The Ephemerides

The biggest takeaway I had from Allison talk, was right at the beginning where she mentions that she does not want to create a Chat bot that is able to mimic human poetry cause that is boring. She wants to go beyond and create a new form of poetry using AI. I find this very interesting because most peoples worries lie in AI taking over human made stuff. Which don't get me wrong is a worry. But not a lot of people think of the ways AI can create new forms of media, new art, new jobs, and new experiences. After watching Allison talk I would like to experiment with creating a piece that uses this idea. Not something that uses AI to mimic a human, but give the agency to the AI guide it to create something that in a way is it's own. Instead of using it, collaborate with it.
